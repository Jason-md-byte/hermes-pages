**How It Works**

The council has five members, each with a distinct personality:

- **The Builder** — obsessed with shipping velocity. If you can't build it in 2 weeks, they want to know why.
- **The Skeptic** — assumes every claim you make is false until proven otherwise. Your market data, your revenue projections, your competitive moat — all suspect.
- **The User** — the only one who cares about whether anyone actually *needs* this. No empathy for technical elegance without real pain.
- **The Investor** — unit economics, runway, CAC-to-LTV ratios. The spreadsheet person.
- **The Strategist** — looks 12 months past launch and asks what you haven't considered.

Each agent reads your plan and attacks from their angle. The output is a brutal, structured critique — and it's exactly what I needed.

**What Got Caught**

My flagship idea was an AI sales coaching tool. I went in confident. The Skeptic went first.

"Your TAM analysis lists three customer segments. That's three times the go-to-market complexity you actually have budget for. Which one is the wedge, and why are you pretending all three are equal?"

It was right. I was padding the numbers to make the total addressable market look bigger.

The council forced me to:

- **Kill two of three planned revenue streams.** They were diluting focus. One core offer, done well.
- **Add formal kill criteria.** Five specific conditions that trigger automatic project shutdown. "If after 90 days we have fewer than 20 warm leads from our ICP, shut it down." Painful to write. Essential to have.
- **Rewrite the financial projections from aspirational to defensible.** The Investor caught three assumptions that were pure optimism, not data.
- **Surface the three risks most likely to actually kill us** — not the generic list of ten unlikely ones that sounds good in a pitch deck.
