# Cold Email Outreach Sequence — Ops-Heavy SMBs

## Overview

**Target Market:** Operations-heavy small-to-medium businesses (SMBs) in four verticals:

| Vertical | Typical Pain Points |
|----------|-------------------|
| **HVAC** | Dispatch chaos, missed service follow-ups, manual scheduling, leads from multiple sources (phone, web, referral) with no central funnel |
| **Roofing** | Long sales cycles, estimate/tracking spreadsheets, poor lead-to-quote conversion visibility, seasonal volume spikes |
| **Dental** | Front-desk overload, new-patient lead management, insurance verification delays, no-show/no-follow-up tracking |
| **Real Estate** | Agent/team lead routing broken, CRM hygiene neglected, transaction coordination gaps, listing lead follow-up lag |

**Common Thread:** Every one of these businesses acquires leads across multiple channels (phone, website, social, referral) and loses 30–50% of them to poor follow-up processes — not because the leads are bad, but because no system exists to catch them.

**Goal of the Sequence:** Move the prospect from awareness of the leak (Touch 1) → belief that a fix is possible (Touch 2–3) → willingness to take a low-friction action (Touch 4) → final decision (Touch 5).

---

## Touch 1 — Problem Awareness

**Subject:** Are leads falling through the cracks at [Company]?

**Body:**
Most ops-heavy businesses lose 30–50% of their inbound leads simply because no one follows up fast enough or in the right way. A roof estimate request sits in email. A dental new-patient form goes to spam. An HVAC dispatch note gets written on a sticky note. These aren't bad leads — they're broken processes. The companies that fix this pipeline don't need more leads; they need a system that holds onto the ones they already have.

**CTA:** Worth a 10-min call to see where yours might be slipping? Reply "yes" and I'll send a calendar link.

## Sending Strategy

| Parameter | Setting |
|-----------|---------|
| **Cadence** | One email every 3 business days |
| **Best send window** | 8:00–10:00 AM local time |
| **Optimal days** | Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday |
| **Avoid** | Monday mornings (inbox flood), Friday afternoons (weekend mode), weekends |
| **Sequence duration** | 15 business days (~3 weeks) |
| **Subject line variation** | Rotate subject lines per touch — no repeated subjects within 30 days |
| **Opt-out** | Unsubscribe link in every email (Touch 5 has opt-out + manual "reply to stay" option) |
| **Tracking** | Open rate baseline: 40–55% expected for this vertical; reply rate target: 8–15% |
| **Follow-up on reply** | If prospect replies with any engagement, move them out of the sequence into a 1:1 sales flow within 24 hours |

**Pro tip for verticals:**
- HVAC/Roofing: Best results Apr–Jun and Sep–Oct (pre-season and shoulder season)
- Dental: Avoid Monday and Friday send days; Tuesday–Thursday midday performs best
- Real Estate: Align with spring (Mar–May) and fall (Sep–Nov) market activity peaks
